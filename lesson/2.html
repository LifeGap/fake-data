Bacalhau com Natas - Portuguese Cod with Cream

A creamy, comforting Portuguese dish made with salted cod, potatoes, onions, and a rich cream sauce, baked to perfection.

Ingredients

  • 500g salted cod (bacalhau), soaked and desalted
  • 600g potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 medium onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 200ml heavy cream (natas)
  • 2 cups milk
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 100g grated cheese (e.g., mozzarella or parmesan)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Pinch of nutmeg (optional)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Cod: Rinse the salted cod under cold water. Soak it in a large bowl of cold water for 24-48 hours, changing the water every 6-8 hours to remove excess salt. Drain and set aside.
  2. Cook the Cod: Place the desalted cod in a pot with the milk. Bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat and cook for 5-10 minutes until tender. Remove the cod, reserving the milk. Flake the cod into small pieces, discarding any skin and bones.
  3. Fry the Potatoes: Heat enough olive oil in a frying pan to shallow-fry the cubed potatoes. Fry until golden and tender, about 10-15 minutes. Drain on paper towels and set aside.
  4. Sauté the Onions: In a large skillet, heat 3 tbsp olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and minced garlic, cooking until soft and translucent, about 5-7 minutes.
  5. Make the Cream Sauce: In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Stir in the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes to form a roux. Gradually whisk in the reserved milk from cooking the cod, stirring constantly until thickened. Add the heavy cream, a pinch of nutmeg, salt, and pepper to taste. Simmer for 2-3 minutes until smooth.
  6. Combine Ingredients: In a large bowl, mix the flaked cod, fried potatoes, and sautéed onions. Pour in two-thirds of the cream sauce and stir gently to combine.
  7. Assemble and Bake: Preheat your oven to 200°C (390°F). Transfer the mixture to a greased baking dish. Pour the remaining cream sauce over the top and sprinkle with grated cheese. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the top is golden and bubbly.
  8. Serve: Garnish with chopped parsley and serve hot, ideally with a side salad or crusty bread.

Notes

  • Soaking time for the cod depends on its thickness—taste a small piece to ensure it’s not too salty.
  • You can use fresh cod if salted cod isn’t available; skip the soaking step and adjust seasoning.
  • Traditional recipes vary—some add olives or skip the cheese, so feel free to tweak it to your taste!
